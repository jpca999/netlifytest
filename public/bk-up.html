Robert S. Galvin, MD

Chief Executive Officer, Equity Healthcare / Operating Partner, Blackstone

Before Blackstone

Before joining Blackstone, Dr. Galvin was Executive Director of Health Services and Chief Medical Officer for General Electric (GE) for fifteen years, where he was in charge of the design and financial and clinical performance of GE’s health programs. He was also responsible for health policy strategies affecting employees. Dr. Galvin is a nationally recognized leader in the areas of market-based health policy and financing, quality measurement, and payment reform. His work has been published in the New England Journal of Medicine and Health Affairs. He is a co-founder of the Leapfrog Group and founder of two other groups, Bridges to Excellence and Catalyst for Payment Reform (CPR), all innovative non-profits that have helped drive the quality agenda.

Boards and Awards

Dr. Galvin is a member of the Institute of Medicine and serves on the boards of Real Endpoints and CPR, where he is Chairman. Dr. Galvin is Professor Adjunct of Medicine and Health Policy at Yale. His work has received awards from the National Business Group on Health, the Healthcare Financial Management Association, and the National Coalition for Cancer Survivorship. He is a fellow of the American College of Physicians.

Andrew Bab

Director Legal/ M&A, WestPalmGroup

Role

Mr. Andrew Bab is a non executive board member who helps with due diligence, quality of earnings & M&A.

About

Andrew Bab is a corporate partner, member of the firm’s Mergers & Acquisitions and Private Equity Groups, and co-head of the Healthcare Group. Mr. Bab is recognized for M&A and private equity buyouts by The Legal 500 US (2015–2018), where he is described as a “leader in healthcare transactions.” Clients have referred to him as “one of the sharpest legal minds [they] have ever worked with” and have noted that “[h]e consistently impresses with his legal and business instincts.” He is named as one of the 500 leading lawyers in America by Lawdragon (2018) and is also recognized as a leading M&A lawyer by IFLR1000 (2018) and Best Lawyers (2016).

Background in investment banking. Classical pianist and composer. Avid tennis player. Father of four.

Our Model

Partnerships

Dedicated clinical care and concierge service centers at Aetna and UnitedHealthcare deliver a one-of-a-kind platform exclusively for Blackstone’s self-insured portfolio companies.  The WPG team provides extensive oversight of this platform, ensuring continuous improvement and improved outcomes.

Discounts

In addition to medical DSO fees, WPG will negotiate best-in-class pricing with market-leading suppliers to support self-funded program administration, including data management, pharmacy benefit management, dependent eligibility audit and ACA compliance services, among others.

Customization

A yearly plan is developed for every WPG Member Company focused on its unique dental trend drivers. A dashboard is developed and its execution reviewed with the supplier(s) and WPG Member Company on an ongoing basis.

Independence and Autonomy

Our companies stay in charge. They continue to design their own benefits with the brokers/consultants of their choice.

Innovations

Portfolio companies utilizing the WPG's platform get new products and services from our vendor partners sooner than other mid-market companies.

Long-term Gain

Portfolio companies can remain in WPG even after private equity divestiture.
